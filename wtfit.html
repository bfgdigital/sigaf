WTF Is This?

The Philosophy: The Bitcoin protocol doesn't care about tweets, regulations, or Elon's dog. Its forward march is relentless. The USD price is just a noisy, human interpretation of this progress.

This tool is an attempt to filter that noise. It uses the fiat price—because we have to—but in a diluted way, converting it into pure, relative momentum. It's not a chart for finding patterns; it's a "fuel gauge" for a quick, objective reading.

How It Works

The Simple Explanation

This tool measures Bitcoin's price momentum using simple geometry inside a "box." It answers the question: "Is the price moving faster or slower right now than its overall historical journey?"

  1. Put It In a Box: Imagine Bitcoin's entire history squashed into a 1x1 square. The x-axis is its age in blocks (0% to 100%), and the y-axis is its price relative to the All-Time High (0% to 100%). This is the great equalizer.
  2. Establish a Macro Benchmark: The tool calculates the "Current Trend Angle." This is the angle of a straight line from the beginning to today's price. This represents Bitcoin's average momentum over its entire life.
  3. Define Micro Horizons: The tool generates five consistent "horizons" — perspectives on the present, ranging from very recent (a few days) to more distant (several months). The spread of these horizons intelligently adapts to the market's overall acceleration, focusing more on the short-term when momentum is slowing.
  4. Measure Momentum Across Horizons: It then calculates the momentum angle for each of these five horizons. Crucially, every single horizon starts at a different point in the past but **ends at the exact same present moment**.
  5. Compare and Assign a Status: The tool compares each micro horizon's angle to the macro benchmark angle. The status tells you how significant that difference is.
    • PARTY: Recent momentum is unusually steep.
    • PEACHY: Recent momentum is moderately steeper than the trend.
    • MEH: Business as usual; momentum is tracking the long-term trend.
    • WATCH: Recent momentum is moderately weaker than the trend.
    • NO BUENO: Recent momentum is unusually flat or negative.
  6. Reach a Verdict: The tool takes a pure, unweighted consensus from the five horizons. If the collective signal is strongly positive or negative, it lets you know.

The Technical Explanation

The meter operates on a 2D coordinate system that is normalized to the current block and the all-time-high price, creating a consistent geometric space for all comparisons.

1. The Normalized Coordinate System (The "Box")

To make geometric comparisons valid, every point (block, price) is mapped to a normalized coordinate (x, y). This is the most important concept of the model.

  • X-Axis (Run): A given block n is normalized by the latest block n_latest. So, x = n / n_latest. The x-axis always represents 0% to 100% of Bitcoin's age in blocks.
  • Y-Axis (Rise): A given price P is normalized by the All-Time High price P_ath. So, y = P / P_ath. The y-axis always represents 0% to 100% of the ATH.

This ensures that a line segment from 10 years ago and a line segment from last week are drawn on the exact same 1x1 grid, making their angles directly comparable measures of velocity.

2. Deriving Lookback Horizons

The horizons are not arbitrary or based on historical correlations. They are generated dynamically based on the character of the current trend.

  1. First, the model fits a binomial (quadratic) curve to the entire normalized price history. This measures the overall curvature of the trend (i.e., is it accelerating or decelerating?).
  2. This curvature then automatically adjusts a "chirped" sine wave generator. The sine wave's frequency changes over its duration.
  3. A flattening price curve (slowing momentum) causes the generator to produce a tighter cluster of more recent horizons, demanding a higher-resolution view of the present.
  4. A steepening price curve (accelerating momentum) produces a broader spread of horizons for a more macro-level confirmation of the strong trend.

3. The Angle Calculation (θ) & Deviation (Δ)

The angle for any price movement is calculated using the arctangent of its slope on the normalized chart. The "signal" is the difference between an angle from a micro horizon (θ_micro) and the overall trend angle (θ_macro). 

// For two points p1 and p2 on the normalized chart:
rise = p2.y - p1.y
run = p2.x - p1.x
θ = arctan(rise / run) × 180/π

// The deviation for a given horizon
Δ = θ_micro - θ_macro

4. The Final Verdict (Pure Consensus)

A simple average or complex weighting would be misleading. Instead, the final verdict is derived from a pure, unweighted consensus of the five horizons.

  • Each horizon casts a "vote" based on its status (e.g., PARTY = +2, MEH = 0, NO BUENO = -2).
  • These votes are summed up. There is no complex weighting. Every horizon's perspective is counted equally.
  • This simple sum determines the final "yep..," "no.," or "yeah nah" verdict, providing a robust and straightforward assessment.

5. The Status (Statistical Significance)

The individual status for each horizon is determined by how many standard deviations (σ) its Δ is from the mean of all five current deltas, making it adaptive to current volatility.

  • PARTY: Δ > +1.5σ
  • PEACHY: Δ is between +0.5σ and +1.5σ
  • MEH: |Δ| ≤ 0.5σ
  • WATCH: Δ is between -0.5σ and -1.5σ
  • NO BUENO: Δ < -1.5σ

A Practical Example: The Crash Test

Imagine the price drops 30% overnight. Because every single angle calculation terminates at the present moment, a catastrophic drop in the current price instantly and simultaneously impacts all metrics on the normalized chart.

  • The Current Trend Angle (our benchmark) would become much flatter, as its end point just dropped significantly.
  • The angle of every recent lookback (Micro Angles) would plummet even more, becoming very low or negative.
  • The comparison—Micro Angle - Current Trend Angle—would result in a large negative number for all horizons, triggering an immediate NO BUENO status across the board.

The entire dashboard would light up with warnings. The consensus would see a unanimous negative vote and deliver a decisive verdict. This happens because the tool isn't showing you five separate historical snapshots; it's showing you five different perspectives on the path to the exact same "now."
