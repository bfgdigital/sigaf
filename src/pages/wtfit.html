What The Fuck Is This Meter?

The Philosophy: A "Fuel Gauge," Not a GPS

The Bitcoin protocol doesn't care about tweets, regulations, or media hype. Its forward march is relentless. The USD price is just a noisy, human interpretation of this progress. Standard charts are filled with patterns that demand interpretation, a process that is often subjective and error-prone.

This tool is an attempt to filter that noise. It's not a chart for finding entry and exit points; it's a "fuel gauge" for a quick, objective reading of market momentum. It asks one question: "Is the protocol's momentum, as measured by price, holding up relative to its entire history?"

The Methodology: From Price to Pure Geometry

To provide an objective answer, we have to escape the distortions of absolute price and time. This model does so by converting Bitcoin's entire history into a single, normalized 1x1 geometric space.

  1. The Great Equalizer (Normalization): Bitcoin's history is squashed into a 1x1 square. The x-axis is its age in blocks (from 0% to 100%), and the y-axis is its price relative to the All-Time High (from 0% to 100%). In this space, a price movement from 2013 can be directly compared to one from last week.
  2. The Benchmark (Macro Trend Angle): We calculate the angle ($θ_{macro}$) of a straight line from the origin (block 0, price $0) to the current point. This single angle represents the average velocity of Bitcoin's entire life.
  3. The Lenses (Micro Horizons): The tool then calculates the angle for five recent timeframes, or "horizons." Crucially, these aren't fixed periods (like "90 days"). They are dynamically generated based on the market's overall character.

The Execution: How the Verdict Is Reached

The final verdict is a pure, unweighted consensus derived from comparing the micro to the macro.

1. Deriving Adaptive Horizons

The five analysis horizons are derived using a method inspired by signal processing to adapt to market conditions:

  • A quadratic curve ($ax^2 + bx + c$) is fitted to the entire normalized price history to measure its overall acceleration or "curvature."
  • This curvature adjusts a formula used to find the peaks of a theoretical chirp signal. This is a signal whose frequency changes over time.
  • If the market is accelerating (a steepening curve), the horizons spread out for a broad view. If it's decelerating (a flattening curve), the horizons cluster more recently for a high-resolution look at the present.

2. Calculating Deviation & Status

For each horizon, its momentum angle ($θ_{micro}$) is compared against the benchmark ($θ_{macro}$). The difference is its deviation ($Δ$). 

Δ = θ_micro - θ_macro

The status (e.g., PARTY, NO BUENO) is assigned based on how many standard deviations this delta is from the average of all five current deltas. This makes the signals inherently adaptive to recent volatility.

3. Reaching an Unweighted Consensus

Each horizon casts a "vote" from +2 to -2 based on its status. These votes are simply summed up. There is no complex weighting. This raw consensus determines the final "Yep..," "No.," or "Yeah Nah" verdict—a robust and straightforward assessment of whether the current momentum warrants your attention.

Visualizing The Geometry

While this tool avoids charts for its final analysis, visualizing the underlying geometry can be illuminating. The chart page demonstrates a core problem with subjective analysis: perspective changes everything.

You will see the macro trend line, which looks steep from a distance, appear nearly flat when you zoom in on the present moment. This distortion is precisely why this meter was built—to replace subjective interpretation with objective calculation.
